## Post 1: The Origin Story

**Platform:** X + LinkedIn

I'm 17 and I just launched an AI company that sells to roofing contractors.

Sounds weird. Here's how it happened:

I noticed that 62% of service calls to contractors go unanswered. Not because they don't want the work — because they're literally on a roof.

Meanwhile, 78% of homeowners hire the first company that responds.

So I built LeadCatch: an AI that texts back every missed call in under 60 seconds, qualifies the lead, and books the appointment.

No app to learn. No staff to hire. The contractor doesn't touch anything.

Price: $2,997/month.
ROI: most clients see 10x in month one.

Building in the most unsexy vertical possible. And I've never been more excited.

## Post 2: The Numbers Post

**Platform:** X + LinkedIn

Week 2 building LeadCatch. Here's what I've learned selling AI to roofing contractors:

- They don't care about AI. They care about booked appointments.
- They don't want software. They want a system that runs itself.
- They don't compare features. They compare ROI.
- The word "automation" scares them. "We handle it for you" sells.
- Their biggest fear isn't the price. It's wasting time on something that doesn't work.

If you're building for blue-collar businesses: stop selling technology. Start selling outcomes.

The pitch that works: "You'll book 2-4 extra jobs per week. If not, we refund your first month."

That's it. That's the whole pitch.

## Post 3: The Technical Deep Dive

**Platform:** X

Tech stack behind LeadCatch (all bootstrapped, $0 VC):

- Twilio for SMS/voice detection
- Make.com for orchestration
- Claude API for AI conversations
- Google Calendar API for booking
- Vercel for hosting
- Stripe for billing
- Brevo for email
- Supabase for data

Total infrastructure cost per client: ~$47/month.
What we charge: $2,997/month.

Margins in vertical AI are insane if you solve a real problem for people who have money and hate technology.

The moat isn't the tech. Anyone could build this. The moat is the 60-second response time, the trained AI persona, and the fact that roofing contractors will never build this themselves.

## Post 4: The Customer Story

**Platform:** LinkedIn

I said: at $12,000 average job value, that's $60,000-$120,000 in potential revenue that called you and got voicemail.

He went quiet for about 10 seconds.

That's the LeadCatch pitch. We don't sell AI. We sell the money they're already losing.

When you do the math for them, the sale makes itself.
