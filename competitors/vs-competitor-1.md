## Where Hatch Wins

Let's be honest about where Hatch has an edge:

- **Multi-channel:** Hatch offers voicemail drops in addition to SMS and email. LeadCatch focuses on SMS and email, which are where 95% of conversions happen for roofers.
- **CRM ecosystem:** Hatch integrates with a wider range of CRMs out of the box. LeadCatch currently supports JobNimbus, Jobber, and custom webhooks on the Scale plan.
- **Lower base price:** At ~$499/mo, Hatch's entry price is lower than LeadCatch's Starter at $597/mo.

---

## Where LeadCatch Wins

- **Speed:** LeadCatch responds to missed calls in under 60 seconds automatically. With Hatch, you need to set up the campaign triggers yourself — and most contractors never do.
- **Zero management:** LeadCatch is fully managed. There are no campaigns to build, no templates to write, no workflows to configure. Hatch requires the contractor (or their marketing person) to manage it.
- **Roofing-specific AI:** Alex is trained on roofing scenarios — insurance claims, storm damage, seasonal urgency, scheduling around weather. Hatch's AI is generic across home services.
- **Storm Surge:** LeadCatch proactively reaches out to homeowners after weather events. Hatch doesn't have this feature.
- **Guarantee:** LeadCatch offers a 30-day money-back guarantee tied to measurable ROI. Hatch does not.

## Who Should Choose What

**Choose Hatch if:**
- You have a marketing person or agency managing your tools
- You want to customize every workflow and template yourself
- You need voicemail drops as a primary outreach channel
- You serve multiple home service verticals (not just roofing)

**Choose LeadCatch if:**
- You're a roofing contractor who wants a system that runs itself
- You don't have time to set up, manage, or optimize campaigns
- Speed-to-lead is your primary concern (60-second response matters)
- You want weather-triggered outreach built in
- You want a money-back guarantee tied to real results
